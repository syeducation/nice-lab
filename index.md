# Welcome to the NICE Research Group

![members of the nice research group, standing in front of a tree](images/lab_2022.jpg)

The NICE Research Group is a collection of researchers in the Department of Psychology
at the University of Minnesota.

The primary emphasis of the NICE Research Group is on metascientific issues in psychology. 
This includes theoretical, methodological, and historical issues, 
as well as scientific reform and open science. 

NICE was originally an acronym that stood for Narrative, Identity, Culture, and Education. 
This reflects the original substantive research focus of the lab, which pertained to 
identity and personality development among racially/ethnically diverse adolescents
and emerging adults. Although we do still continue some of that work--particulalry
with external collaborators--it is no longer the primary emphasis of the group. 
Thus, "NICE" is a dead acronym that no longer stands for anything other than we 
try to be generally good people, even when we are criticizing other researcher's work.
